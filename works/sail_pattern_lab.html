As part of the Sensory & Ambient Interfaces Lab at WashU, we are exploring technology-enabled test protocols to measure, categorize and qualify different haptic patterns.

Building on past work with active haptic feedback systems that communicate performance data during athletic training, our team is developing a web application to evaluate how haptic patterns affect brain waves.

Research Approach

We're exploring technology-enabled test protocols to measure, categorize and qualify different haptic patterns. The goal is to understand how different tactile feedback patterns affect cognitive performance and brain activity.

Key Findings

Initial studies show promising results in using haptic patterns to enhance focus and reduce cognitive load during complex tasks.
